REMOVABLE PROSTHETICS

A removable dental prosthetics are dental restoration that can be removed by the patient when it is not been used. A dental prosthetics is an intra-oral (inside mouth) prosthetics are used to restore (reconstruct) intra-oral defects such as missing teeth, missing parts of the teeth, and missing soft or hard structure of the jaw and palate.

Prosthodontics is the dental specialty that focuses on dental prostheses, such prostheses are used to rehabilitate mastication (chewing), improve aesthetics, and aid speech.

A dental prosthesis may be held in place by connecting it to the teeth or dental implant by suction, or by holding it passively to its surrounding muscles. Like other types of prostheses, they can either be fixed permanently or removable. Fixed Prosthodontics and removable denture are made in many variations.

Examples of Dental prostheses
  • Dentures.
  • Partial dentures.
  • Palatal obturator.
  • Orthodontics appliances.
  • Dental implant.
  • Crown.
  • Bridges.
History of Dental prosthetics

Many methods was disclosed to replace lost teeth using substitutes made from caved blocks of ivory or bone, and dental braces was introduced to correct the position of teeth using gold wires and silk threads.

Conditions related to Dental Prosthetics
  • Bias-phosphate associated osteone crosis of the jaw.
  • Bruxism.
  • Edentulism.
  • Occlusal trauma.
  • Tempormandibular joint disorder.
Types of Removable Dental Prosthetics
  1. A fully removable denture: It is fixed to the gum.
  2. A partial removable denture: Fixed to abutment teeth if they are present.
  3. A conditionally removable denture: More secure fixation, more comfortable for the patient. It is required to insert an implant, which will act as a support for a bridge or crowns.
Materials used for Removable Prosthetics

Most commonly, removable partial denture (RPD) retainer are fabricated identically from the metal frame works alloy as cobalt chromium (CoCr) alloy although it is un-aesthetic.

Types of Dental Prosthetics

However, other prosthetics can be used in certain conditions, but these are the four (4) main types usually being considered:

  • Trans-radial.
  • Trans-femoral.
  • Trans-tibial.
  • Trans-humeral.
Benefits of Removable Prosthetics
  1. Unlike implants or bridges, dentures are removable for easy cleaning and storage.
  2. Removable dentures are one of the most inexpensive ways to replace missing teeth.
  3. The removable denture restores functions to your mouth and can also help preserve the contour of your face. Also porcelain dentures provide excellent aesthetics since replacement teeth looks a lot more like natural teeth. Moreover, the lesion associated with removable prosthetics is Denture Stomatitis, it is the most common condition which affect the palatal mucosa of wearers of complete or partial removable dentures in about 50% of it.
Reasons to consider Removable Prosthetics

Specific reasons why people consider (RDP) include:

  • Cost: Removable dentures are often a fraction of the cost of dental implants.
  • Flexibility to add teeth.
  • Ability to save natural teeth.
  • Non-invasive procedure.
Disadvantage of Removable Prosthetics
  • Adjustment period: There is a small adjustment period needed to get used to the feel of removable denture.
  • Maintenance.
  • Bone loss.
  • Denture must be taken out at night.
  • Plaque buildup.
Hygiene of Removable Prosthetics

Proper hygiene of dental prosthetics is the first step to avoid conditions such as tartar, gingivitis, or periodontal disease. The prosthetics like the natural denture accumulate bacterial, even more due to the retention point they have with existing denture.
