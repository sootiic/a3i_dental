SPORT APPLIANCE

What is Sport Appliance?

A sport appliance is made of flexible plastic and fit over the top arch of teeth. The mouth guard cushions a blow to the face, reducing risk of broken teeth, as well as injuries to the jaw, tongue, lips, and cheeks.

What is Mouth Guard?

Mouth guards are covering worn over teeth, and often used to protect teeth from injury from teeth grinding and during sports. There are three types of mouth guards:

  1. Stock mouth guards: These are performed and they are ready to use. They are also expensive to purchase at any sport store or sport department and in other departmental store.
  2. Boil and bite mouth guards: These are widely available at stores which sell sport equipment. Boil and bite mouth guard are made out of a special thermoplastic material. It is put into hot water to make it soft, and then put in the mouth and formed so that it is shaped around the teeth.
  3. Custom fit mouth guards: This is the one of the most expensive option in mouth guards because; they are designed and created in dental offices or in a professional lab. The dentist will make an impression of the teeth and the mouth guard is molded to fit perfectly.
Advantages

Wearing a mouth guard while you sleep can help reduce your risk for:

  • Wear and tear from clenching or grinding your teeth.
  • Snoring.
  • Jaw pain, headaches, facial pain and other symptoms of TMJ disorder.
  • Sleep apnea and related complications, including high blood pressure and daytime fatigue.
Disadvantages
  • Getting addicted: Patients typically get used to the dental device.
  • Improper fit: If your sport guard does not have a proper fitting, it can slip off your mouth mainly during teeth grinding.
  • Not built for long-term use.
  • Not following proper instructions.
